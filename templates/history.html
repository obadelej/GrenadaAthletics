History of Grenada Athletics Association

A Century of Excellence and Achievement

Our Founding

January 11, 1924 - The Grenada Amateur Athletic Association was established

The Grenada Athletics Association (GAA), originally founded as the Grenada Amateur Athletic Association, was established on January 11, 1924. It is the governing body for athletics in Grenada and became affiliated with the International Association of Athletics Federations (IAAF) in 1970. The founding of the association occurred during a period of significant social and political changes in the British Caribbean colonies and was part of a broader movement where sports federations were formed alongside colonial administrative changes.

Early Years and Challenges

For many years, athletics struggled to gain widespread public attention in Grenada, a country more captivated by cricket. Early athletics competitions were limited by poor infrastructure, such as rudimentary grass tracks, which hampered the development of athletic talent. The first Grenadian athlete to gain regional recognition was William "Pablo" Gittens in the 1950s, who excelled in various events including high jump, long jump, and 400m hurdles.

International Emergence

Grenada's international athletics presence began to grow notably in the 1960s and 1970s, marked by participation in the Commonwealth Games starting in 1970 and joining the Central American and Caribbean Championships. Athletes like Dunstan Campbell, who won a bronze medal in decathlon, were part of this early international emergence.

Olympic Debut

Los Angeles, 1984 - Grenada's first Olympic appearance

The association has witnessed significant milestones, including Grenada's Olympic debut in Los Angeles in 1984, sending a seven-athlete delegation. Over the decades, the country has produced globally recognized athletes such as Alleyne Francique, Kirani James, and Anderson Peters, enhancing the profile of athletics in Grenada.

Celebrating a Century

January 2024 - 100 Years of Excellence

Celebrating its centenary in January 2024, the Grenada Athletics Association holds a proud legacy of nurturing athletic talent and contributing to the international athletics community, despite initial struggles with limited facilities and public interest. It continues to be a central figure in promoting, organizing, and developing athletics in Grenada.

Our Enduring Legacy

From humble beginnings with grass tracks to producing world champions, the Grenada Athletics Association has transformed the landscape of athletics in our nation.

Today, the GAA continues to build on its rich heritage by providing modern facilities, comprehensive training programs, and opportunities for athletes to develop their skills and compete at the highest international levels. We remain committed to upholding the highest standards of the sport and inspiring the next generation of Grenadian athletes.
